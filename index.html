Chang

Chang, a cherished Newari liquor, is a traditional fermented beverage deeply rooted in Nepal's cultural heritage. This ancient drink, often enjoyed during festive occasions and communal gatherings, is crafted from barley, millet, or rice. The brewing process involves fermenting these grains with a unique blend of natural ingredients, resulting in a refreshing, slightly effervescent brew with a distinctive, earthy flavor profile.

Typically served chilled in traditional Newari vessels, Chang has a cloudy appearance with a rich, frothy head. Its taste can range from mildly sweet to tangy, depending on the specific recipe and fermentation period. This artisanal liquor not only embodies the essence of Newari craftsmanship but also serves as a symbol of hospitality and communal spirit in the vibrant Newar community. Whether savored in a festive setting or enjoyed in casual company, Chang offers a genuine taste of Nepal's rich cultural tapestry.

Choila

Choila is a beloved Newari dish renowned for its vibrant flavors and rich culinary tradition. Originating from the Newar community of Nepal, Choila is a spicy and savory preparation featuring grilled or roasted meat, commonly pork, buffalo, or chicken, though variations with other meats can also be found.

The meat is first marinated in a fragrant mix of spices, including cumin, coriander, fenugreek, and chili, then grilled to perfection. After grilling, the meat is finely chopped and tossed with a tangy, spicy mixture of roasted spices, garlic, ginger, and often a splash of lime or lemon juice. Fresh herbs such as cilantro or mint and sliced onions are also added to enhance the dish's complexity and freshness.

Typically served at room temperature or slightly warm, Choila is often accompanied by traditional Newari sides like beaten rice (chiura), pickles (achar), and sometimes a hard-boiled egg. This dish is a staple at celebrations and special occasions, celebrated for its bold flavors and its role in bringing people together. With its tantalizing heat and robust taste, Choila is a true reflection of Newari culinary artistry.

Supu Mhicha

Supu Mhicha is a distinctive and traditional dish from the Newar cuisine of Nepal, celebrated for its unique flavor and hearty texture. This traditional Newari delicacy features a rich, flavorful soup made from a combination of meat and vegetables, often including ingredients like goat or buffalo meat, lentils, and a variety of aromatic spices.

The preparation begins with simmering the meat and lentils together, allowing the flavors to meld and the broth to become deeply savory. The soup is then enriched with a fragrant blend of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and fenugreek, alongside ingredients like garlic, ginger, and sometimes tomatoes. Fresh herbs and vegetables are added to complete the dish, imparting a vibrant, robust flavor profile.

Supu Mhicha is typically enjoyed as a comforting and warming meal, often accompanied by steamed rice or traditional Newari breads. It's a popular choice during cooler months or special occasions, embodying the essence of Newari hospitality and culinary tradition. Its satisfying, complex flavors make it a cherished part of the Newar culinary heritage, reflecting the skill and tradition of its preparation.
